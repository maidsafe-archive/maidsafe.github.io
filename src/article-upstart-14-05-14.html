Net neutrality’s false choice and a third option that could revolutionize the Internet

Upstart
14 May 2014

The UpTake: The decentralized network first made popular by bitcoin has many other applications, including the Internet itself.

We are being presented with two choices in the argument for so-called “net neutrality,” or the idea that every website should have equal access to the same Internet speeds. Tomorrow, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) is expected to make a decision as to whether Internet Service Providers can charge for faster speeds, which would give a competitive advantage to richer companies.

The choice we are being presented with is between an Internet with stronger FCC government protections that limit the power of Internet Service Providers (ISPs) like AT&T, Comcast, and Cox Communications, or a free-market Internet dominated by these massive companies which would be free to charge whatever the market lets them get away with.
